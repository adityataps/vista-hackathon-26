### What is the focus of this year's Vista Hackathon?

The 2026 Vista Hackathons challenge teams to build a complete, working application powered by **agentic AI orchestration**. All teams will receive a unified challenge prompt and work toward a shared problem space, making team composition and technical readiness the decisive factors.

### What do we mean by "agent"?

An agent is an AI system designed to independently carry out multi-step tasks by perceiving its environment, reasoning about context, and taking purposeful action with minimal human intervention.

Agents exhibit the following core characteristics:

- **Autonomy:** Operating without continuous human guidance to pursue a defined goal
- **Reasoning:** Evaluating context, trade-offs, and available information to make decisions
- **Memory:** Retaining and recalling prior interactions to inform future behavior
- **Learning:** Updating behavior based on feedback and environmental signals
- **Tool Use:** Leveraging APIs, plugins, databases, external software, and other digital services to gather information, trigger workflows, or take action

### What is agentic AI orchestration?

Agentic AI orchestration refers to the coordination layer that governs how one or more agents collaborate, sequence tasks, delegate subtasks, and manage state across a multi-step workflow. An orchestration layer determines which agent handles which part of a problem, how results are passed between agents, and how the overall system progresses toward a goal.

### What is Model Context Protocol (MCP)?

Model Context Protocol (MCP) is an emerging open standard that enables AI models to communicate with external tools, data sources, and services in a consistent and interoperable way. MCP defines how an agent requests context, invokes tools, and receives structured results. Teams building integrations with external systems should be familiar with MCP as a foundational pattern for agentic architecture.

## Participation and Team Composition

### Can companies participate in both Vista Hackathons?

Yes. Company participation in both the North America and Global Hackathons is allowed and strongly encouraged. However, individual attendees and teams should plan to participate in only one event.

### What roles must be on a team to qualify?

Teams are **required** to participate as fully formed pods that include all five of the following roles. Participants may serve multiple roles (e.g., a PM may also fill the UX designer role). Teams that do not meet this composition requirement will not qualify:

1. **Product Manager** — Voice of the product and customer. Deep knowledge of company strategy, competitors, core workflows, CX design, and pricing/packaging.
2. **User Experience Designer** — Hands-on knowledge of the company's end-to-end user workflows and established design patterns. Ensures the solution is grounded in real user needs.
3. **Architect or Tech Lead** — Senior engineer with command of existing architecture patterns and demonstrated experience designing agentic/AI-native system architectures. Ensures technical soundness and scalability.
4. **Engineer** — Hands-on expertise in building agentic applications, including agent frameworks, orchestration patterns, and the team's chosen technology stack. Leads implementation.
5. **DevOps or Cloud Engineer** — Comprehensive knowledge of cloud operations, dev environment config, and AI/agentic services from the team's cloud provider. Ensures deploy, run, and demo within the event window.

> [!info] Team Size
> Each team must include **4–5 participants** from their individual company. HBCU student participants are additional and do not count toward this total. One participant must be designated as the **team lead**. Team rosters are final once the onsite event begins — no additions or substitutions.

### Why is this team composition required?

Building a complete agentic AI application within a 24-hour window requires genuine depth across product, design, architecture, engineering, and infrastructure. This structure is consistent with how successful agentic product teams are built in practice.

### How are teams and participants selected?

Each company's leadership team nominates attendees. The five-role pod structure is required for qualification. Vista may limit the number of teams per organization based on event capacity.

### How are teams formed?

Teams are formed internally at individual Vista companies. Vista will match HBCU students with participating teams at the North America event.

## Project Guidelines

### What will teams be working on at the event?

Teams will receive a unified challenge prompt at or ahead of the event, focused on building a complete agentic AI-powered application. All teams work from the same prompt — domain expertise, technical preparation, and cross-functional collaboration are the key differentiators.

### What do teams need to prepare before the event?

> [!warning] No Pre-Building
> Participants may **not** begin development work prior to the official event start. This includes prototyping, coding, or building any functional components.

Teams **may**:

- Set up cloud credentials
- Install required software and development tools
- Configure development environments and CI/CD pipelines
- Validate access to their company's cloud and AI services
- Ensure access to various data sources from remote locations
- Configure any coding agents (Claude Code, Cursor, etc.) to assist in rapid development

> [!danger] Disqualification
> Pre-building any application logic, UI components, or data pipelines is not permitted. Vista will implement judging controls to assess whether project work was completed within the event timeframe. Submissions showing evidence of pre-event development will be disqualified.

### Can teams work on a product or feature already under development?

**No.** Projects under active development or already included in an organization's commercial product suite are not eligible.

### How will Vista verify that project work was completed during the event?

Vista will use an automated agent to review each team's code repository. The agent will assess commit history, timestamps, and contribution patterns to verify development occurred within the official event window.

### What are the technical requirements for submissions?

All submissions must leverage **agentic AI orchestration** as a core component of the solution.

### What is the role of Responsible AI in this year's event?

Participants must submit a **Responsible AI Assessment** as part of their hackathon submission.

- Responsible AI = developing, deploying, and managing AI systems in an ethical, transparent, accountable manner aligned with human values
- Involves designing systems that respect privacy, ensure fairness, promote inclusivity, and prevent unintended harm
- Critical to product development — maximizes benefits while minimizing potential harms and fostering trust

### What are the post-event expectations for hackathon projects?

Companies should be prepared to evaluate their submitted project for potential inclusion in their product roadmap. CTOs/CPOs may be asked to present the final disposition at the next regularly scheduled board meeting.
